Ngwe Saung Beach-Myanmar

Ngwe Saung Beach, meaning "Silver Beach" in Burmese, is one of Myanmar’s most popular coastal retreats, located about five hours’ drive west of Yangon. With its long stretch of soft silver-white sand, calm blue waters, and gently swaying palm trees, Ngwe Saung offers a refreshing balance between the lively buzz of Chaung Tha and the quiet luxury of Ngapali. The beach stretches over 15 kilometers, making it one of the longest in Southeast Asia, perfect for long walks, beach cycling, or simply unwinding on a hammock under the sun. During weekends and holidays, local and international travelers alike flock here to enjoy the sea breeze and vibrant beachside atmosphere. It’s an ideal spot for families, couples, or young travelers seeking adventure and relaxation in equal measure. The beach is clean and well-kept, and though more developed in recent years, it still retains a laid-back and charming village feel. A highlight for many is the small Lovers’ Island located just offshore, which can be reached by foot during low tide. Adventurous visitors can try banana boat rides, jet skiing, snorkeling, or even take a boat trip to explore the nearby islands. The coral reefs near the rocky ends of the beach offer glimpses of marine life, though they are modest compared to those in other Southeast Asian countries. A wide range of accommodations—from modest beach bungalows to mid-range resorts—line the coast, offering comfortable stays without the higher price tag of Ngapali. Dining options are also diverse, with beachfront restaurants serving fresh seafood, local Myanmar dishes, and even international cuisine, all at reasonable prices. As evening falls, bonfires and candle-lit dinners create a relaxed, romantic ambiance under the stars. Vendors are friendly and not overly persistent, and local motorbike rentals make it easy to explore nearby fishing villages or ride along the coastal road. With its combination of natural beauty, affordability, and growing range of tourist facilities, Ngwe Saung is fast becoming a favorite beach destination for those looking to enjoy Myanmar’s coastline without straying too far from the country’s major cities. Whether you’re after peaceful sunsets, fun water activities, or simply a soothing seaside break, Ngwe Saung offers a memorable experience along the shores of the Bay of Bengal.

Top Attractions

Lovers Island

Lover’s Island is a sleeping islet on the coast of Ayeyarwady Region’s Ngwe Saung Beach. According to the locals, the name Lover’s Island is based on the mythology of star-crossed lovers expatriated from the mainland only to show off their affection among the lush foliage 500 meters from shore. However, Lover’s Island is actually a name given to foreigners since foreign couples often visit this place to have a private space. When the tide is out, parts of Bengal Bay reveal a passageway of sea-floor to Lover’s Island.

Bird Island

The island, commonly known as Bird Island, is located about 9 nautical miles from Ngwe Saung Beach. The main reason why it is called Bird Island is because there were Edible-nest swiftlet birds on the island. Bird Island is a type of rocky island, and there is no sandy soil on the island. If you go up to the mountain of the bird island, you can also visit the bird’s nest cave, where the birds live. If you go by speedboat, it may take about 50 minutes round trip. If you go by local boat, it can take about 3 hours round trip.

Things To Do

RELAX ON THE BEACH

It is an amazing experience to soak up some sun and immerse into the ocean.

WALK TO LOVER'S ISLAND

Lover’s island is at the end of Ngwe Saung Beach. It offers panoramic views of the beach from its summit. It also offers snorkeling opportunities amongst the coral and brightly colored fish under the waters. One thing that makes the island special is that it is accessible by foot. When the tide is low enough, just take a walk along the beach.

SNORKELING AT BIRD ISLAND

Bird island is one of the famous islands in Ngwe Saung. It takes one hour to get there by boat from the shore. It is a perfect place for snorkeling thanks to crystal clear water and large diverse marine lives

VISIT SINMA FISHING VILLAGE

Sinma fishing village is a great place to explore the daily life of fishermen. Every night, the fishermen go out into the sea for fishing and return home the next day. In the early mornings, we can see the fishing boats coming in with their day’s catch. Stroll along the beach, passing the vast straw mats which are used to dry small fish, meet the fishermen and buy their freshly dried products. It offers great photo opportunities to capture this scenery.
